Ethics & Legal in Marketing

Consumer Protection: The FTC, CPSC, and other agencies set standards to ensure consumer safety and fair competition. These regulations cover product safety, financial transparency, and truthful advertising.

Advertising must be:

  • Truthful
  • Avoid Manipulation
  • Be Culturally Sensitive

Unethical examples which mislead customers might include:

  • Fear-based advertising
  • Greenwashing
  • Stealth Marketing

Channel Management

A process used by companies to direct and manage various marketing techniques and the parties involved in distribution.

Modern marketing techniques will use:

  • Inventory control software: Tracks the amount, kind, and value of inventory. Typically uses barcodes or radio frequency identification (RFID) tags to identify and track inventory
  • Partner relationship management software: sales and marketing processes, partners, including recruitment, training, and compliance of channel members
  • Channel analytics software - Analytical data transformation
  • Channel data management software for point of sale data from customers
  • Incentives and compensation management software, for rebates, loyalty programs, etc.

Benefits include inventory management and organization of logistics, allowing product quantities to be available in the right place, at the right time.
Organized channel management structures will also account for quick product recalls as well as secure communication between channels.

Ethical channel management centers on fairness, transparency, and respect for partners. This includes fair treatment, open communication about policies and pricing, and respecting partner autonomy without excessive control. Ethical practices also require avoiding conflicts of interest, safeguarding partner data, and promoting environmental responsibility through sustainable practices. Continuous support and improvement are also key, providing resources and addressing issues constructively to maintain strong, trust-based partnerships.


Pricing Function

"Price is what you pay, value is what you get" -Warren Buffet

A marketing function that involves the determination of an exchange price at which the buyer and seller perceive optimum value for a good or service.

Pricing must be fair and not exploit customers or undercut competition. Practices like price-fixing, predatory pricing, and price discrimination are illegal, while others like price skimming must be transparent. i

Factors Affecting Pricing:

  • Costs
  • Supply and demand
  • Economic Conditions
  • Competition
  • Government Regulation
  • Channel members & Objectives

How pricing affects the marketing mix:

  • involve determining what goods, services, or ideas to produce or sell that will satisfy customersâ€™ needs and wants.
  • affects the type of research conducted, the length of the research project, and the amount of money spent on research.

Ethical pricing focuses on fairness and transparency. It involves setting prices that reflect the true value of products without exploiting vulnerable groups and clearly communicating all costs. Businesses should avoid price discrimination and ensure that discounts are genuine. They must also consider social responsibility by making essential goods affordable for low-income customers, especially during crises, and avoid price gouging during emergencies to prevent exploitation.


Role of Promotion

The role of promotion in marketing includes the following functions:

  • Creating awareness: Informing target audiences about the merits of a product, service, brand, or issue.
  • Generating interest: Making people intrigued and interested in your offer.
  • Building an image: Establishing a distinctive place in customers' minds.
  • Encouraging action: Convincing buyers to take action, such as purchasing or trying a product.

Pricing affects promotion decisions in the following ways:

  • Choice of medium via promotion: Getting the lowest-cost use of medium compared to maximum exposure
  • Amount of money spent in promotional budgets
  • Time/Company resources allocated to the promotion
  • ways that companies ship their products to their final destination
  • Where the product is offered

Technologies used may include:

  • Social Media Platforms: Channels like Facebook, Instagram, and LinkedIn, offering paid ads, content sharing, and audience targeting.
  • Email Marketing Software: Tools like Mailchimp and Constant Contact for personalized email campaigns and automated outreach.
  • Search Engine Marketing (SEM) and Optimization (SEO): Google Ads for paid search and tools like Ahrefs or SEMrush for organic traffic optimization.
  • Content Management Systems (CMS): Platforms such as WordPress and HubSpot to publish, manage, and promote content.
  • Analytics and BI Tools: Google Analytics, Tableau, and other tools to track campaign performance, conversions, and customer insights.
  • Influencer Marketing Platforms: Services like AspireIQ and Upfluence connect brands with influencers to expand reach.
  • Customer Relationship Management (CRM): CRMs like Salesforce enable targeted promotional outreach based on customer data.
  • Video and Multimedia Tools: Platforms like YouTube and Vimeo for video ads, and tools like Canva and Adobe Spark for promotional content creation.



Ethical promotion requires truthful and fair marketing practices that respect the target audience. Promotions should provide accurate information, avoid deception, and be transparent about terms and conditions. Businesses must engage in fair competition and ensure that promotional content does not promote harmful behaviors or stereotypes.

Legal promotions must comply with advertising regulations set by entities like the Federal Trade Commission (FTC), which mandate truthfulness and prohibit deception. Companies should respect intellectual property rights, adhere to consumer protection laws, and follow privacy regulations when using customer data. They must also ensure that endorsements are truthful and disclose any material connections.

Selling Function

Involves communicating with potential customers and pursuing sales leads.

Sales representatives play an important role in activities such as prospecting, presenting, and closing sales. They can use different types of techniques to cater to their customers' preferences. Modern selling will change with enhanced adaptability and personalization, as AI and social media algorithms become more effective.

Sales reps will use these techniques:

  • Prospecting: Identifying potential customers and generating leads. This involves researching potential buyers and reaching out to create sales opportunities.
  • Pre-approaching: Gathering information about prospects before contacting them. This step helps tailor the sales approach to the customer's specific needs and interests.
  • Making presentations: Presenting product or service information to potential customers in a persuasive way, focusing on the benefits and solutions the offering provides.
  • Handling objections and complaints: Addressing any concerns or objections customers may have, providing solutions, and ensuring that customer concerns are resolved satisfactorily.
  • Closing sales: Finalizing the sale by encouraging the customer to make a purchase decision, often by offering incentives or asking for the order directly.
  • Following up: Checking in with customers after the sale to ensure satisfaction, address any further needs, and build long-term relationships for future business.

In order to analytically be effective, techniques such as the following must be used:

  • Detailing: Providing thorough and detailed information about the product to emphasize its features and benefits, ensuring customers understand its value.
  • Communicating the Product's value: Clearly articulating how the product solves specific customer problems, meets needs, or adds value to their lives.
  • Email marketing: Using targeted email campaigns to nurture leads, share relevant information, and keep potential customers engaged with the brand.
  • Prioritize high-value online leads: Focusing efforts on leads that are most likely to convert, often identified through data analysis and lead-scoring systems.



Technologies used in selling:
Selling utilizes various technologies to enhance efficiency and customer engagement. Key tools include Customer Relationship Management (CRM) systems like Salesforce and HubSpot for managing customer data and interactions. E-commerce platforms such as Shopify and WooCommerce facilitate online transactions, while sales enablement tools like Outreach and SalesLoft streamline communication. Analytics tools provide insights into customer behavior and sales performance, enabling data-driven decision-making. Mobile apps and social media are crucial for targeted advertising and direct customer communication.

Ethical & Legal impliations of selling:
In selling, ethical implications include ensuring honesty and transparency, avoiding deceptive tactics, and respecting customer privacy. Sales representatives must not pressure customers and should provide accurate product information. Legally, businesses must comply with regulations protecting consumer rights, including truth-in-advertising laws and data protection regulations. Companies should respect intellectual property rights, adhere to anti-fraud laws, and ensure sales practices are fair and non-discriminatory.

Marketing-Info Management

Data Management: Marketers must handle customer data carefully to avoid misuse. Laws like COPPA, the CAN-SPAM Act, and the Telemarketing Sales Rule regulate data usage and communication, requiring companies to disclose practices, protect consumer privacy, and follow ethical standards.

Also emphasize maintaining data accuracy to avoid misleading conclusions, ensuring transparency about data collection practices, and avoiding manipulation of customer data to prevent exploitation of vulnerabilities.

On the technology side, marketing information management uses tools such as Customer Relationship Management (CRM) systems like Salesforce and HubSpot to manage customer data. Data analytics tools like Google Analytics help analyze marketing performance and customer behavior. Market research tools such as Qualtrics gather customer feedback, while Data Management Platforms (DMP) assist in organizing data for targeted marketing. Additionally, Business Intelligence (BI) tools provide insights for decision-making, and social media analytics evaluate engagement to inform marketing strategies.
